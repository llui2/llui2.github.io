Cycle holonomy induces higher-order constraints and controls remote synchronization transitions via twisted Laplacian spectra, arXiv preprint, April 2026.

A spectral framework connecting remote synchronization transitions to cycle-level topological constraints. We model edge phase lags as a $U(1)$-valued connection, show how cycle holonomy obstructs synchronization through the twisted Laplacian spectrum, and derive transition thresholds for frustrated phase-locked states.

Indirect influence on network diffusion, Communications Physics, Volume 8, Article 494, December 2025.

A framework for measuring how indirect pathways change diffusion timescales in networks. We use the $d$-path Laplacian to define indirect influence, derive perturbative and mean-field predictions for random networks, and identify the structural transition where non-local paths start to reshape diffusion.

Structural prediction of super-diffusion in multiplex networks, Chaos, Solitons & Fractals, Volume 186, September 2024, 115265.

A structural approach to predicting when diffusion on a multiplex network becomes faster than on any individual layer. We introduce a predictor based on minimum node strength, derive analytical bounds for super-diffusion, and show how inter-layer connection patterns can induce the effect.
